PROJECT IDEA #4

Another project idea has found us. Winter series. Timbo Vest (by Gabriella Calderini) The Timbo Vest is a simple yet interesting knit, suitable for beginners and fast enough to become your go-to gift project. It is worked from top down in an easy broken rib stitch, with a stockinette shoulder detail. A free pattern on Ravelry. We are currently working on a sample for in the store. Decided to make the neckline just a few rows instead of the full turtleneck style. Knitted in chunky yarn it will move along quickly for you. I love the idea of it keeping my body warm while allowing my arms to be free. Make it as long or as short as you wish!
Free pattern on Ravelry! Worked top down. Is in the round after armhole sections are completed. Make it in 2 colours or one solid. Either will look nice. We have chosen our new Eco Tweed chunky to knit the sample.

Level - advanced beginner

Sizes: XS to XL

Materials

- chunky weight yarn
- check pattern for quantities depending upon size
- Needles: 6.5mm in 40, 60 & 80(or 100)cm circular (all 3 lengths required)
- Gauge: 15sts/22 rows = 4in in broken rib pattern

PROJECT IDEA #3

This is more of a project idea than a kit. The 3rd in our winter series. Southwood (by Galt House of Yarn) is a unisex pullover. A free pattern on Ravelry. Top down with a few short rows at the back of the neck (pattern says front but we know it's the back!), raglan sleeve increases and a straight body without any waist shaping. I am currently knitting this for my son-in-law in Juniper Moon's Rustic Aran. Colourway: 1009 Bayleaf. If you follow us on Instagram or Facebook you may have seen my knitting updates. It's going pretty fast. This is a good first/second pullover pattern for any knitter. I have provided links to the pattern page (click pattern name above or on the photo) and a link to some yarns that I think would make a great sweater for you or the man in your life.

A simple top-down raglan pullover in a full range of sizes. Baby (0-6mons) right up to 5X. Southwood now includes options for both a plain raglan neckline (great for beginners!) or with added short-row shaping for a more contoured neckline.

Level - advanced beginner
Sizes: 0-6mons up to 5XL

Materials
- aran or worsted weight yarn
- check pattern for quantities
depending upon size - Needles: 4.0mm x 60cm circular, 5.0mm x 60cm & 80cm, DPN's/30cm circulars in 4.0 & 5.0 for sleeves
- Gauge: 18sts/24 rows = 4in in stocking stitch

PROJECT IDEA #2 - USONIAN SHAWL

Several visitors to our webpage have commented on the Usonian Shawl. It was featured in our "What are We Knitting Now" section. So we have decided to kit it with colour combinations and the pattern to make it. I think you will enjoy this pattern. Knit now & have it ready to tuck inside your coat for winter/spring walks. The Usonian is a contemporary striped shawl with a striking combination of textured and lace stitch patterns.The asymmetrical triangular shaping begins with just a few stitches which then flow into striations of eyelets, garter bands, striped ribbing, twisted stitches on reverse stockinette and a woven ribbons lace border.

- Level - advanced beginner

Materials

- combinations of fingering weight yarn
- 1 skein of each colour
Preferably a light & dark for contrast
- we are offering 7 great colour combinations
- Needle: 3.75mm x 80cm circular & a 4.5mm of some variety for the cast-off
- Gauge: 18sts/4in in stocking stitch
- Usonian pattern
