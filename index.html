Keep on Knitting !

Lots of knitters think that once winter is over, so is knitting season. Not true! While your focus may change (hey, who wants to knit a heavy blanket in July anyway?) you can still go on knitting during the warmer months. I choose this time to make more socks and smaller or lightweight items. There are also an incredible array of spring/summer yarns and patterns out there. And even though I don't like to say it, Christmas is always "just around the corner". Particularly for hand-knitted items. Start planning your gifts now. You can work on smaller pieces and purchase yarns on sale or during yarn crawls. Get ahead of the game so you aren't feeling the pressure in September!


Sideways Tee by: Churchmouse Yarns & Teas

This deceptively simple top is cleverly knit from the center out to each side, rather than from top to bottom or vice versa. A modern pivot to a familiar style! A tapered shape allows for plenty of ease at the bust, while providing a relaxed and flattering bottom edge at hip length.

We are suggesting Jody Long's Alba or Estelle's Eco Tweed DK for a slightly tweedy wool oversweater. Giving you warmth over a turtleneck/ blouse for springtime walks.

Or think of Creative Lazy Hazy Summer Cotton DK , 8 great colours to choose from & a slight ripple texture to keep it interesting

Shearwater Socks by Sandra Buzza

This is a very basic top down sock pattern with a beginner friendly textured stitch that gives a subtle blending of colours to soften the look. The instructions are written for magic loop but can be easily converted to double pointed needles.

We are suggesting you try out our Pro Lana Bamboo Socks . A no-wool sock alternative that is wonderful to wear for summer. You can make these regular length or shorties, just by adjusting how long you knit the leg portion.

Free pattern on Ravelry



Old River Road by: Kay Hopkins



A traditional Henley pullover with a modern twist. It’s the perfect pullover to wear with everything. Picture it over a linen dress or with your favorite jeans and boots. Timeless style and total comfort!

Easy textured stitch pattern. You can choose your hemline finish too & even do the contrast finish on the Henley button placket.

Construction is top-down & in the round in a completely seamless manner. The yoke & body are worked back & forth until the front Henley opening is closed & then the body is worked in the round.

We are suggesting Queensland United for a great light-weight cardi.

a pay pattern on Ravelry



Tanke Top by: Ankestrick

Worked seamlessly from the top down. An interesting knit, construction wise. Beginner friendly. Optional pockets and hood.

This pattern is looking for a fingering weight yarn and we are thinking that Berroco's Remix Light is just the ticket. A beautiful lightweight yarn made up of nylon, cotton, acrylic, silk & linen. It has a soft cottony feel. Huge selection of colours to help you make it your own.

PROJECT IDEA #8 - The Boulevard Blanket

The Boulevard Blanket….it’s quick, easy, chunky, warm and reversible! At first glance you might think this pattern is just simple garter stitch. Look again!
An easy K3, P3 repeat pattern creates a subtle striping effect. The name of this pattern is inspired by the beautiful tree lined boulevards of my hometown….Kansas City.
Free pattern by: Fifty Four Ten Studio on Ravelry. Excellent beginner project. Nicer than just plain garter stitch but still fast to knit - https://www.ravelry.com/patterns/library/the-boulevard-blanket.
Level Beginner
Sizes: Baby to 2XL (6sizes)

Materials
- superbulky yarn
- check pattern for quantities of main colour depending upon size
- Needles: 9.0mm x 32 inches
- Gauge: 18.5sts/14.5 rows = 4in in garter stitch

PROJECT IDEA #7 - Novice Sweater Junior

The Novice Sweater Junior is knit seamlessly from the top down. That means that you will begin by knitting the neckband, then the yoke, then the body, and finally the sleeves. The sweater is knit in stockinette stitch, while the neckband, bottom edge (waist band), and cuffs are knit in rib.
Purchase pattern by: PetiteKnit on Ravelry. Excellent beginner project. She used lace weight + fingering + fingering to achieve chunky weight. You can just use a chunky yarn to make the knitting easier. - https://www.ravelry.com/patterns/library/novice-sweater-junior.
Level Advanced beginner
Sizes: 1-2yrs to 12-14yrs
Materials
- check pattern for quantities of main colour depending upon size
- Needles: 5.5mm & 6.0mm varying lengths for neckline, body, sleeves & cuffs
- Gauge: 16sts/21 rows = 4in in stocking stitch
