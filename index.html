Knitting Inspiration

Knitters are always looking for ideas. These four patterns will hopefully help you choose something for your next project. If only we had enough time !

Fever Dreams Shawl by: Kate Burge & Rachel Price


An intermediate level pattern that features a simple slipped stitch dot motif and an interesting and easy to memorize honeycomb stitch. You can create it with a main and 3 accent colours or we think a main an 1 colour changing yarn might do very well. Two sizes, 68" or 94" (purchase yarn accordingly) We are suggesting Highland Alpaca Fine or Alpaca Merino Fine or Berroco Vintage Sock (it's super soft) for the main colour. You can choose your 3 contrasting colours from these as well. Or perhaps Laines du Norde Paint Sock for some fun honeycombs.

Pattern is $7.00USD on Ravelry

Korshaven Sweater Junior by: Tonje Hodne


This has to be the sweetest little basketweave sweater I've seen in a long time. In a huge size range of 3 months to 14 years you can knit one for everyone! Originally knit in a 100% wool together with a mohair strand, we are thinking of something softer and easier to care for. Vintage DK offers a wide range of colours in a superwash (machine wash, flat to dry). Other choices are Alpaca Merino DK, Highland Alpaca DK, Eco Tweed DK (hand wash) or Cherished DK(anti-pilling, 100% acrylic, machine wash & dry) for this knit. Perfect for boys or girls.

Pattern is $8.60 CAD on Ravelry

Waterrock Slipover by: Jennifer Kelley


I'm kind of enchanted with the slipover idea right now. Keep your body warm but free your arms to do what you want. This pattern offers a wide size range , is knitted in the round and has a great little side hem slit to make it sit just right. Knitted bottom-up you can easily adjust the length when you get near the underarm area. DK weight means the fabric should be fairly light. We are thinking Juniper Moon's Patagonia, Eco Tweed DK, Wool Pop (fab yarn and it's on sale!),

Glacier - Dubble(Double) Mittens by: The Weststrand Sisters


Lined mittens that fit snugly and are warm even in colder temperatures. A classic ski mitten was the inspiration. The outside of each mitten is decorated with a texture pattern reminiscent of a glacier surface. Requires an aran/worsted for the exterior and a sport for th interior. We suggest Eco Shetland Worsted, Santa Cruz or Vintage Worsted for outer and Drops Alpaca on the inside.

Pattern on Ravelry $6.75CAD

PROJECT IDEA #9 - Carmel Pullover

The Carmel pullover is knitted bottom-up & in the round.

Sizes: 32 to 68"
Materials
- check pattern for quantities of main colour depending upon size

PROJECT IDEA #8 - The Boulevard Blanket

The Boulevard Blanket….it’s quick, easy, chunky, warm and reversible! At first glance you might think this pattern is just simple garter stitch. Look again!
An easy K3, P3 repeat pattern creates a subtle striping effect. The name of this pattern is inspired by the beautiful tree lined boulevards of my hometown….Kansas City.
Free pattern by: Fifty Four Ten Studio on Ravelry. Excellent beginner project. Nicer than just plain garter stitch but still fast to knit - https://www.ravelry.com/patterns/library/the-boulevard-blanket.
Level Beginner
Sizes: Baby to 2XL (6sizes)

Materials
- superbulky yarn
- check pattern for quantities of main colour depending upon size
- Needles: 9.0mm x 32 inches
- Gauge: 18.5sts/14.5 rows = 4in in garter stitch

PROJECT IDEA #7 - Novice Sweater Junior

The Novice Sweater Junior is knit seamlessly from the top down. That means that you will begin by knitting the neckband, then the yoke, then the body, and finally the sleeves. The sweater is knit in stockinette stitch, while the neckband, bottom edge (waist band), and cuffs are knit in rib.
Purchase pattern by: PetiteKnit on Ravelry. Excellent beginner project. She used lace weight + fingering + fingering to achieve chunky weight. You can just use a chunky yarn to make the knitting easier. - https://www.ravelry.com/patterns/library/novice-sweater-junior.
Level Advanced beginner
Sizes: 1-2yrs to 12-14yrs
Materials
- check pattern for quantities of main colour depending upon size
- Needles: 5.5mm & 6.0mm varying lengths for neckline, body, sleeves & cuffs
- Gauge: 16sts/21 rows = 4in in stocking stitch
