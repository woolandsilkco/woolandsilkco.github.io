Call the store to sign up (519) 925-6194

Hitofude by: Hiroko Fukatsu
Purchase pattern on Ravelry.
As implied by the name “Hitofude”, meaning a single brush stroke in Japanese, this open front cardigan with an allover lace pattern is worked in one “yarn stroke” -- a continuous strand without cutting the yarn.
The yarn stroke starts from the upper body and sleeves worked together in a rectangular piece, then moves to lower body, where the lace pattern increases evenly, creating a gentle drape.

Level - intermediate
Sizes: 30 to 54"

Materials
- fingering weight yarn (2 to 4 skeins)
- check pattern for quantities
- Needles: a 3.5mm x 80cm circular, 1 additional 3.5 for 3-needle bindoff, crochet hook, waste yarn & stitch markers
- Gauge: 20sts/32 rows = 4in in lace pattern

Shop Fingering Weight Yarns

Frank Shawl - KAL

by: Joji Locatelli
A very easy and stunning shawl, inspired by the work of artist Frank Stella, this design is a study in geometry.

Triangular pieces are assembled together to create a spectacular wrap with a unique shape that is both beautiful and stunning to wear, with many tips, all in different shades.

I played with a gradient of grays, and my favorite color, gold, to give this shawl a vintage vibe.

The Frank Shawl is constructed as 3 triangular sections, joined together when working the last one of them.

Skill level: Beginner

FINISHED MEASUREMENTS

97” (244 cm) from side to side and 39” (98 cm) from top to bottom at deepest point.
See Schematic at the end of the pattern.

Yarn:
Takes (4) skeins of fingering weight yarn

PROJECT IDEA #7 - Novice Sweater Junior

The Novice Sweater Junior is knit seamlessly from the top down. That means that you will begin by knitting the neckband, then the yoke, then the body, and finally the sleeves. The sweater is knit in stockinette stitch, while the neckband, bottom edge (waist band), and cuffs are knit in rib.
Purchase pattern by: PetiteKnit on Ravelry. Excellent beginner project. She used lace weight + fingering + fingering to achieve chunky weight. You can just use a chunky yarn to make the knitting easier. - https://www.ravelry.com/patterns/library/novice-sweater-junior.
Level Advanced beginner
Sizes: 1-2yrs to 12-14yrs
Materials
- check pattern for quantities of main colour depending upon size
- Needles: 5.5mm & 6.0mm varying lengths for neckline, body, sleeves & cuffs
- Gauge: 16sts/21 rows = 4in in stocking stitch

PROJECT IDEA #6 - Kukka Cardigan

The Kukka Cardigan Chunky is a classic open front cardigan. It is cut closely to the body but not tight-ly and, knitted from fluffy yarns, fits wonderfully under a winter jacket. If you work the cardigan in a fluffy, light yarn, it is perfect for the transition time in spring and autumn. The Kukka Cardigan Chunky is worked flat top down with raglan increases. The sleeves are worked in rounds with the Magic Loop method, double pointed needles or any other needle system of your choice. The front bands are worked with slip stitches and create the typical “Kukka look” together with the easy to remember rice grain pattern.
Purchase pattern by: rosa p. on Ravelry. Worked top down & in the round seamlessly. You can knit it in a chunky yarn, with or without added mohair. Or in a soft chunky like Estelle Drift. - https://www.ravelry.com/patterns/library/kukka-cardigan-chunky.
Level Intermediate (or advanced beginner)
Sizes: XS to 5XL
Materials
- check pattern for quantities of main colour depending upon size (766-1640 yds)
- Needles: 6.0mm & 6.5mm varying lengths for neckline, body, sleeves & cuffs
- Gauge: 14sts/23 rows = 4in in stocking stitch
